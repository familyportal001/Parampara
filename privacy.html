Last updated: March 2026  ·  Applies to Parampara web application

03

Why we collect it and legal basis

Under UK GDPR and the EU GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing personal data. Our processing activities and their legal bases are:

Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b))

We process your email address, name, and account credentials to provide the Parampara service — specifically to authenticate you, identify you to other members of your family tree, and enable collaboration. Without this data, the service cannot function.

Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f))

We retain edit history (timestamps and user IDs attached to changes) to allow family admins to review edits and maintain data quality. This is proportionate and does not override your privacy interests, given that this data is only visible to admins of families you belong to.

Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

Photos you upload to people profiles are processed only with your explicit action. You can remove photos at any time by editing the person's profile.

04

Who can see your data

Other family members

Members of a family tree you belong to can see the people and relationships in that tree, including content you added. Your display name is visible to all members.

Family admins

Admins of a family tree can also see your email address, your role, your join date, and the edit history showing which changes you made.

No third-party sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third parties for marketing, advertising, or analytics purposes. Your data is not visible to other families on Parampara.

Legal disclosure

We may be required to disclose data if compelled by a court order or other legal obligation under UK law. We will notify you where legally permitted.

05

Third-party services

Parampara uses Google Firebase (a Google LLC product) to store and process data. Firebase operates under Google's data processing terms and stores data in the EU (europe-west1, Belgium) region.

Firebase is covered by Google's Standard Contractual Clauses for international data transfers. You can review Google's privacy terms at firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

We also load fonts from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com). This causes your browser to make a request to Google's servers when you load Parampara. Google may log the font request. If you prefer to avoid this, you can use a content blocker; the app remains fully functional with system fonts.

No other third-party services, analytics platforms, advertising networks, or tracking tools are used.

06

How long we keep your data

We retain personal data for as long as you have an active account and are a member of at least one family.

  • Deleting your account removes your Firebase Auth credentials and member records from all families immediately. Family tree content (people and relationships you added) remains in those trees for other members unless the family admin deletes the entire tree.
  • Leaving a family removes your member record from that family. Your contributions to that family's tree are preserved.
  • Inactive accounts are not automatically deleted. You may delete your account at any time via Account & Privacy in the app menu.

If you wish to have specific content removed from a family tree (such as information about you added by another member), please contact the family admin or contact us at the address in section 1.

07

Your rights

Under UK GDPR (and EU GDPR where applicable), you have the following rights:

Right to access
Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
Right to rectification
Correct inaccurate data. You can update your name directly in the app.
Right to erasure
Request deletion of your account and personal data.
Right to portability
Export your family tree data as JSON via Account & Privacy in the app.
Right to object
Object to processing based on legitimate interests.
Right to restrict
Ask us to pause processing of your data while a dispute is resolved.

To exercise any of these rights, use the Account & Privacy menu in the app, or contact us at privacy@parampara.app. We will respond within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

If you are dissatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

08

Security

All data is transmitted over HTTPS (TLS). Passwords are hashed by Firebase Authentication — we never store or access your plaintext password.

Access to family data is controlled by Firebase Security Rules, which enforce role-based permissions at the database level. Only authenticated members of a family can read that family's data.

Email verification is required before a new account can access any family data, reducing the risk of unauthorised access via typo'd email addresses.

No security system is perfect. If you discover a vulnerability, please report it to privacy@parampara.app.

09

Cookies and local storage

Parampara does not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies.

We use browser localStorage (not cookies) to store:

  • Your language preference
  • Session information linking your account to your family trees (stored locally to avoid repeated Firebase lookups)
  • Dismissed banners and onboarding state
  • An offline snapshot of your family tree for faster loading and offline use

This data lives only on your device and is not transmitted anywhere other than back to Firebase to keep your session alive. You can clear it by clearing your browser's site data for this origin.

Firebase Authentication uses its own cookies to maintain your signed-in session. These are strictly necessary for the app to function and do not require consent under UK GDPR's "strictly necessary" exemption.

10

Children

Parampara requires users to be at least 13 years old to create an account, in accordance with Firebase Authentication's terms of service.

Family tree profiles (the "people" in the tree) may include data about children and minors — for example, a family might record a child's name and date of birth. This data is entered by adult members and is only visible to other approved members of the family. We do not use this data for any purpose other than displaying it within the family tree.

If you believe a child's data has been recorded without appropriate consent, please contact us and we will work with the family admin to address it.

Continued use of Parampara after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised terms. If you do not agree, you may delete your account at any time via the app.
