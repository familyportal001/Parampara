01

Who we are and how to contact us

Parampara is a private, collaborative family tree application. The operator ("we", "us", "our") is the individual or organisation that deployed this instance of Parampara and is the Data Fiduciary as defined under the DPDP Act, 2023 — the entity that determines the purpose and means of processing your personal data.

For any privacy-related questions, requests, or complaints, please contact us:

Privacy contact
Email: parampara.support@gmail.com
We aim to respond to all requests within 7 days, as required by Rule 14 of the DPDP Rules, 2025.
02

What personal data we collect

We collect only the data necessary to provide the service.

Category Data collected Source
Account data Email address, password (hashed by Firebase Auth — we never see the plaintext) You, on registration
Profile data First name, last name, optional profile photo You, on registration
Family tree data Names, dates of birth, dates of death, gender, occupation, biography, photos of family members you add You, while using the app
Technical data Device/browser type, IP address (Firebase infrastructure logs), app errors (in-memory only, not transmitted) Automatically

We do not collect Sensitive Personal Data beyond what you voluntarily enter into family tree fields (e.g. dates of birth). We do not collect financial data, government IDs, biometric data, or health data.

03

Why we collect it and lawful basis

Under the DPDP Act, 2023, we must have a lawful basis for processing personal data. We rely on the following:

Consent (Section 6 of the DPDP Act)

When you register, you explicitly agree to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy. This constitutes your free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous consent for us to process your personal data for the purposes described in this policy. You may withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account (Settings → Account & Privacy → Delete Account). Withdrawal of consent will result in the deletion of your account and removal from all family trees.

Legitimate uses (Section 7 of the DPDP Act)

We also process certain data without separate consent where it is necessary to:

  • Perform the contract with you (providing the family tree service you signed up for)
  • Comply with legal obligations under Indian law
  • Protect a person's vital interests (e.g. preventing serious harm)
  • Respond to a medical emergency involving you or a family member

Purposes of processing

  • Creating and managing your account
  • Displaying your family tree to approved family members
  • Sending email verification and password reset emails (via Firebase Auth)
  • Notifying family admins of pending member approval requests
  • Providing offline access via your device's local storage (IndexedDB)
  • Diagnosing and fixing technical errors
04

Who can see your data

Other family members

Approved members of the same family tree can see the names, photos, dates, and other tree data of all people in that tree. This is the core purpose of the app.

Family admins

The admin of your family tree can see your name, email address, and role. They can approve, demote, or remove members.

No third-party sharing

We do not sell, rent, share, or disclose your personal data to any third party for commercial purposes. Your family data is never shared outside your family tree's approved members.

Legal disclosure

We may disclose personal data if required to do so by a court order, government authority, or applicable Indian law. We will notify you where legally permitted.

06

How long we keep your data

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purpose it was collected:

  • Account data: until you delete your account
  • Family tree data: until the family is deleted by the admin, or until you leave the family
  • Profile photos: until you remove them or delete your account
  • Firebase Auth logs: retained by Google per their own retention policy

You can delete your own account at any time via Settings → Account & Privacy → Delete Account. This immediately removes your account from Firebase Auth and your membership from all family trees. Family tree content you contributed (names, dates, relationships) may remain visible to other members unless the entire family is deleted.

The family admin can delete the entire family tree (Settings → Manage Family → Delete Family Tree), which permanently removes all people, relationships, and member data for that family.

07

Your rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act, 2023 (Chapter III) and the DPDP Rules, 2025, you have the following rights. We will respond to all valid requests within 7 days.

Right to access
Request a summary of the personal data we hold about you and the purposes for which it is processed.
Right to correction
Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. You can also update most data directly in the app.
Right to erasure
Request deletion of your personal data. Use Delete Account in Settings or contact us at parampara.support@gmail.com.
Right to withdraw consent
Withdraw your consent to processing at any time. This will result in account deletion as consent is the basis for the service.
Right to data portability
Export your family data as a CSV at any time via Settings → Export Family Data. This includes all people and relationships.
Right to nominate
You may nominate another individual to exercise your rights on your behalf in the event of your death or incapacity.
Right to grievance redressal
If we fail to honour your rights, you can file a complaint with the Data Protection Board of India.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at parampara.support@gmail.com. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may file a complaint with the Data Protection Board of India once it becomes operational. Details will be available at the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) website: meity.gov.in.

08

Security

We implement reasonable security safeguards as required by Section 8(5) of the DPDP Act, 2023:

  • Passwords are hashed by Firebase Authentication — we never store or access your plaintext password.
  • All data in transit is encrypted using TLS (HTTPS). The app enforces HTTPS exclusively.
  • Firebase Security Rules enforce role-based access — members can only read data from their own family tree.
  • Email verification is required before a new account can access any family data.
  • Data at rest is encrypted by Google Firebase infrastructure.

Data breach notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify you and report to the Data Protection Board of India in accordance with Section 8(6) of the DPDP Act, 2023 and Rule 7 of the DPDP Rules, 2025, within the prescribed timeframe (72 hours where possible). The notification will include the nature of the breach, data affected, likely consequences, and steps taken to address it.

09

Children

Under the DPDP Act, 2023 (Section 9), a "child" means any individual who has not completed 18 years of age.

Parampara is a service intended for adults (18 years and above). We do not knowingly allow individuals under 18 to create an account. If we discover that a user under 18 has registered without parental consent, we will delete their account promptly.

Adding children to the family tree: You may add biographical data about children (under 18) as entries in the family tree. This data is visible only to approved family members. We do not use this data for advertising, tracking, profiling, or behavioural monitoring, in compliance with Section 9(3) of the DPDP Act.

If you believe a minor's data has been added to a family tree without appropriate consent, please contact us at parampara.support@gmail.com.

10

Cookies and local storage

Parampara does not use advertising cookies or tracking cookies. We use browser storage for the following strictly necessary functional purposes:

  • localStorage — stores your language preference, session data, and dismissed onboarding notices. No personal data is stored in localStorage.
  • IndexedDB — stores a local cache of your family tree data for offline access. This data stays on your device and is only synced to Firebase when you are online.
  • Service Worker cache — caches the app shell (HTML, CSS, JS, fonts) for fast loading and offline use. No personal data is cached by the service worker.

Firebase Authentication may set a session cookie to maintain your login state. This is strictly necessary for the service to function.
